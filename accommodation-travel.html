Travel guide

Supervisors, chairs, delegates,

The conference will be held at our Prague 1 school campus, 'Valdštejnská 151 / 6a', which is located in a majestic palace building directly under the walls of the Prague Castle, close to the Malostranská metro stop (line A - green), and the stops of tram lines number 8, 12, 18, 20 and 22 are all just one to two minutes walk from the school.

Our opening and closing ceremonies will take place in a breathtaking venue named Pražská křižovatka (kostel sv. Anny) also known in English as Prague Crossroads, found at the ‘old-city-centre’ side of the river, street address Zlatá 211. It is within walking distance from Charles Bridge, a must-visit destination and is easily accessible, just a three minute walk away from the tram stop Karlovy lázně, tram lines number 2 and 18. Pražská křižovatka is also eight minutes away from the Staroměstská metro stop (line A - green). Václav Havel, Czech playwright and political dissident who, after the fall of communism, was the president of Czechoslovakia was a driving force for the establishment of the Pražská křižovatka. His vision was to create a welcoming environment for a wide range of events, including meetings, lectures, conversations, concerts, performances, exhibitions, occurrences, and meditations, while taking into account the world's global variety. The major goal of these gatherings was to address the now generally recognized need for an open and creative dialogue among people of many religions, views, and professions about the condition of our civilization, the threats it faces, and its hopes and future. It is an undoubtedly ideal venue for our PLISMUN’22.

We feel honoured to warmly welcome you to our conference from the very heart of Prague. All our venues are located in the absolute city center and bring the full historical atmosphere, every visitor longs for, right to you. For people travelling longer distances from hotels, we recommend you use Uber, AAA Taxi, Bolt or Liftago taxi services. We are looking forward to seeing you!
